01 — The title race

George, Jon, Lainie, Gabe —
and now Lainie again.

Every line is one forecaster’s running point total. George Hilaiel led from the opening whistle; Jon’s +11 on Matchday 6 ripped the throne away and held it six days. Then Lainie — the cup’s most conservative scorer — went on a tear (a record +15 on MD12, then a third lone perfect on MD13) and ran the lead out to eight. On Matchday 14 the Commissioner answered: Gabe’s league-record +17 across a six-game slate seized the top. Matchday 15 dialed up the chaos — Germany, Japan and the USA all failed to win — and Gabe clung on by two. Then Matchday 16 flipped it again: Lainie answered with a second +15 (a perfect Egypt 1–1 among it) to retake the throne, while down in the basement Lloyd finally climbed off the bottom. Then a draw-soaked Matchday 17 played into the Matriarch’s hands — a +16, banking both draws nobody else called, ballooned her lead to nine. Then the knockouts arrived with double points: Lloyd briefly rocketed onto the podium with a doubled Canada 1–0 — before Lainie blew the race open, nailing the Netherlands–Morocco shootout to the goal (the new ladder’s 6-point top tier) for a doubled, league-record +22. The lead is out to 29 and still climbing — 31 after Matchday 20. Since then it’s become a rout — a blind, no-research +24 on Matchday 23 (a league record) pushed the lead to 45, and held it there through the Round of 16. Then came the Azteca: on a night Norway shocked Brazil and England edged Mexico 3–2, Lainie posted a rare blank — yet the lead barely dented, still 40, because the chasers were too busy trading places to gain on her. Lloyd’s perfect England 3–2 — the only one to call it (Jake had it, then changed it) — leapfrogged him past Gabe into 2nd by a single point, while Jake’s +8 rocketed the scorekeeper from last to 5th. Four lead changes — and a runaway leader over a field that will not stop reshuffling behind her.

09 — The rulebook

How a point is won

Locked in before the opening game — and quietly overhauled the day the knockouts arrived, when a match can end on penalties.

2 pts
Right result — you called the winner, or the draw.
3 pts
Right result + one team’s exact score.
5 pts
The perfect scoreline, nailed on the nose.
New for the knockouts — when it goes to penalties
